RFQ Entry - Vendors

 

DB Distributor > Request for Quotation System > Processing > RFQ Entry - Vendors (RFQ A1 #2)

 

The vendors screen is initially used for entering in the quantity breaks you would like to have quoted, and selecting the vendors to be included in the quote. After the quotes are returned, you can return to this screen to enter in your quoted costs. Note: You do have the option of having your vendors enter in their own quotes through the use of the eCommerce toolkit. Please refer to the How To section for more information on this.

 

Follow these steps to complete the panel for the initial setup:

  1. Across the top, enter in up to 5 different quantity breaks that you would like to have quoted. This will be per the "cost" unit of measure that you entered in during step 3 of the lines section.
  2. Click the Add Vendor button to select individual vendors to add to the quote, or click the Use Vendor Group button to add a vendor group code from a list that you have previously setup.
  3. If you chose the Add Vendor option, you will be taken to a screen where you can select the vendor, and then add any specific comments that you would like to add for that vendor. You can also designate if you need the quote physically sent to them or not. All other fields will be left blank at this time. If you chose the Use Vendor Group option, you can double-click on any of the vendors returned to get to these same options.
  4. After all of your appropriate vendors have been added to the quote, you can press the Next button. This will return you to the lines section. From there you can press the Add button to add additional lines, or press the Done button to return to the header section and save your quote.

 

Follow these steps to complete the panel when entering quoted costs:

  1. When the vendor quotes come back in they will need to be entered in on this screen. To enter in their quoted costs, simply double-click on any vendor to bring up their data screen.
  2. Enter in their quoted costs under each quantity that they quoted. Note: This needs to be the cost per the unit of measure, not the total cost for the quantity.
  3. If the quote is from a particular plant, enter in which plant it is coming from.
  4. If the vendor assigned a reference number to their quote, enter in that quote number.
  5. Repeat steps 1 through 4 for all vendors on the quote that returned their quotes. Once all quoted costs are entered, click the Show Best button. This will select the best priced vendor for each quantity tier that was quoted. Note: You have the option of manually selecting a different vendor on any quantity break.
  6. You will then have 3 ways of determining your sell price for each quantity tier. You can enter in a gross profit dollar amount for each quantity break. You can enter in a gross profit percent in the first column, which will default to the other columns as well. The last option would be to manually enter in your desired sell price per the unit of measure across the bottom row. You can also use a combination of these methods as well.
  7. Once you have determined your pricing structure, you can exit the quote, and proceed to the Customer Quote Output option to send out a customer quote letter. You are also ready at this point to turn this quote into an order through the Order Processing module.
