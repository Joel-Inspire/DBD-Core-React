Journal Entry

What is a Journal Entry? To answer this, let's first define a "Journal".

A Journal is a chronological listing of transactions of a similar type that prove the point of entry into the accounting system.  Therefore, a Journal Entry is simply an entry into a Journal.  If you were operating within the General Ledger System only, all of your transactions would be entered as direct Journal Entries.   However, as TopForm is a fully integrated accounting system with other accounting applications, other journals will directly post to your General Ledger. Your only direct Journal Entries will be to record depreciation, accruals and other adjusting entries.

Information Flow for Journal Entries

 

At this point, lets see how information flows within the General Ledger System.

 

Entering a Journal Entry

 

  1. Choose the Journal Posting Folder from the General Ledger Menu to enter your Journal Entry data.

  2. Choose the General Journal Entry Option and enter your journal entries.

  3. Choose the General Journal Report Option and print this report.

  4. If necessary to correct any errors or enter other journal entries, repeat the preceding steps.

  5. Update the General Journal.

 

The General Journal Update does the following:

  • Creates details postings in the Daily Transaction Register File

  • Clears the General Journal Entry File
