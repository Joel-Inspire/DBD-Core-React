Concepts and Practices

Before getting into the details of the system, it might be helpful to investigate how information flows within the TopForm General Ledger. Once a company has been set up, there are two major processing areas, Daily and Periodic Processing (Month-end and/or Year-End).

Daily Processing

 

There is actually very little daily work to be done in your General Ledger System, as it receives most of its information from your other accounting applications. For example, when you enter invoices or produce checks in your Accounts Payable System, the appropriate General Ledger postings are forwarded to the General Ledger. The same is true for Accounts Receivable. When you input sales and cash receipts the General Ledger effect is recorded automatically.

The only direct entries in General Ledger are Journal Entries.

 

Daily Processing Involves

 

  • Creating New General Ledger Account Numbers

  • Entering and Updating Journal Entries

  • Updating the Detail Transaction Register

 

Periodic Processing

 

Periodic Processing primarily consists of making any adjusting or recurring entries, printing your financial reports and then performing Period End Processing.

 

  • Recurring Journal Entry Processing

  • General Ledger System Reports - Although any of the General Ledger reports may be printed at any time, even subsequent to period end, it is common practice to print a hard copy of the financial reports and save these reports with your other accounting records.

  • Period End Processing
