Recent Year End - Beginning Balances Available

If your fiscal or accounting year-end has just recently passed, you may want to convert as of your prior fiscal year end. For instance, if you were implementing the system in October or November, and your fiscal year ended September 30, it might be advantageous to capture the transactions on at least a summary basis starting October 1.  

 

The procedure to accomplish this would be:

 

  1. Enter the year ending balances as of September 30 for your Balance Sheet Accounts. It would not be necessary to enter balances for the Income and Expense Accounts, since they are closed at year end to retained earnings.

  1. Select the General Ledger Parameter Setup to set the Fiscal Year and Accounting Period to the year and period for which you are beginning the conversion.

  2. If you have not already done so, using the Fiscal Year Setup Program, set up any prior fiscal year records for which you will be entering information.

  3. Select the Journal Entry program.

  4. Be sure the Fiscal Year and Accounting Period are properly set. If the program does not accept the Fiscal Year, chances are the prior fiscal year record is not set up.

  5. Use a "JE" Source Journal Code.

  6. Enter "Ending Balance Conversion" for the Description.

  7. Enter the account number and ending balance for all non-zero balance sheet accounts. (Be certain to enter a minus sign before or after the amount when entering credit balances.) Don’t forget to include your year-to-date net income in your Retained Earnings Account.

  8. Print the General Journal, verify it, and update

 

Print the Detail Transaction Register and Update.

 

Print a Financial Statement for the period and review for accuracy. Remember, Assets must equal Liabilities and Owners Equity.

  2. Enter summary postings for activity since the year end using the Journal Entry program.  Or, if feasible, enter the actual transaction within the Accounts Receivable or Accounts Payable System.  (Refer to the conversion section on each application before using that application.)

 

This method would allow you to have a General Ledger with correct balances for each accounting period within the current fiscal year.
