Bank Statement Data - Deposits

 

DB Distributor > Bank Reconciliation > Processing > Bank Statement Data > Checks (B/R P1 [F6])

 

This is the area of the bank reconciliation where you will come to designate your deposits as being "cleared" or "outstanding".

 

Follow these steps to complete the panel:

 

  1. Enter in a deposit number to clear, or use the lookup to see a listing of deposits that have been processed through the Cash Receipts program.
  2. On the right side of the screen, you can designate if the deposit has been cleared, or if there is a discrepancy. When you click on "Yes" or "No" for the cleared field, the next deposit record will automatically be brought up. If you marked the deposit as a "discrepancy", proceed to the next step.
  3. If you marked the deposit as having a discrepancy in the previous step, a field will appear where you can enter in the amount that the bank recorded for the deposit. There is also a field where you will enter in if the error was a bank error. The way that you answer this question will determine if the difference of funds will show in the G/L Adjustments field, or the Plus/Minus Bank Errors field. Note: GL Adjustments will post to the account you specified in step 7 of your Bank Account setup. If there is a minor discrepancy, a general ledger posting is usually sufficient to correct the mistake. However, any discrepancies that affect a customer account must be corrected through Accounts Receivable.
  4. After matching all of your deposits from the statement, press the Exit button.
