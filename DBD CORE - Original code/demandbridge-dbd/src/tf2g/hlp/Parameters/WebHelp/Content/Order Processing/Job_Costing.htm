Inventory Job Costing

 

What it does:

  • Provides for the accumulation of cost over time on Bill as Shipped Inventory
  • Adjusts remaining lots as additional costs are received
  • Allows for invoicing of customer before all costs are received
  • Allows cost of special charges to be bundled into inventoried item

 

Requirements:

  • Applies to Bill as Shipped Inventory Only
  • Only one “Custom Item” per order - must be entered on line one.
  • Special Shipping Point must be “1” (not location code, but shipping point)
  • Product must be initially shipped to only one distributor warehouse
  • Inventory must be tracked as lotted
  • Cannot delete any lines on the order once the product has been received

 

There is a one-time setup in order to use this feature. In the AR Sales Distribution Codes, you will need to setup and identify an Inventory in Process account.

 

Example of a Bill as Shipped order using the Inventory Job Costing feature:

 

A bill as shipped order is placed for 10 Cases of Item X. The item has a cost of $100 per case, and is on line 1 of the order.

Line 2 is a special charge for artwork, with an estimated total cost of $50.

Line 3 is a special charge for freight, with an estimated total cost of $75.

 

Event 1 - Vendor invoice for the special charge of artwork arrives. The line is received through PO Receiving. The cost of $50 is posted to the Inventory in Process GL account, and the transaction is recorded into the job cost transaction file, since there is no inventory to post the charge to.

Event 2 - The 10 cases of item X arrive without the vendor bill. They are placed into inventory with a total cost of $1,000 for the item itself, plus $50 for the artwork, plus the estimated $75 cost of freight, for a total inventory cost of $1,125. Note: even though the freight line has not been received at all, all estimated costs on the order will apply to the inventory cost.

Event 3 - 3 cases of Item X are shipped to the customer with the cost at $112.50 per case. (So at this point, $337.50 of cost has shipped on the 3 cases, and $787.50 total cost remains on the 7 cases in inventory.)

Event 4 - The vendor bills arrive for Item X and for the freight. Item X is billed by the vendor at $105 per case, and the vendor bill for freight comes in at $77.50. There is a total of $52.50 additional cost than was estimated. This additional $60 of cost will go into the total cost on the lot of 7 cases remaining, taking its total cost from $787.50 to $840.00.

So while the initial 3 cases went out with a cost of $112.50 per case, the final 7 cases will go out with a cost of $120.00 per case. These average out to the true cost per case of $117.75.
